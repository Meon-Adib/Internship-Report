WHAT IS INDUSTRIAL TRAINING

Industrial training is a structured program that allows students to gain practical experience in a real working environment. It enables students to apply the theoretical knowledge learned in the classroom to real-world situations related to their field of study. Industrial training also helps students understand professional work culture and prepares them for their future careers.

IMPORTANCE OF INDUSTRIAL TRAINING

  • Provides students with real-world working experience.
  • Helps students apply theoretical knowledge in practical situations.
  • Improves problem-solving and communication skills.
  • Prepares students for future careers in their respective industries.

OBJECTIVES OF INDUSTRIAL TRAINING

  • To expose students to the real working environment and workplace atmosphere.
  • To apply the theories learned at the university in solving real-world problems related to their respective fields of specialization.
  • To provide exposure to knowledge and the latest technological developments used in the industry.
  • To foster and strengthen networking and collaboration between the university and industry.

OBJECTIVES OF THE INDUSTRIAL TRAINING REPORT

  • To train students to prepare work documents effectively.
  • To develop students who are responsible for the work they perform by preparing documents related to their tasks.
  • To expose students to the proper methods of preparing documents.
  • To build students’ self-confidence when preparing documents.
  • To fulfill the mandatory requirement for students to pass Industrial Training.
